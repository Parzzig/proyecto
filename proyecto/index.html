The Legend Of Zelda
History

No kingdom. No memories. After a slumber that has lasted 100 years, Link awakens alone in a world he no longer remembers.

Now, the legendary hero must explore this vast, mysterious world and recover his memories before Hyrule is lost forever.

Armed only with what he finds in his path, Link will embark on an adventure to search for answers and obtain the resources he needs to survive.

the world and its history

Armed only with uncertainty, Link ventures into the merciless loneliness of a transformed Hyrule. Between majestic mountains and grassy valleys.

This is how the story unfolds, under the vast sky of Hyrule, where the absence of memories is not just a void, but the blank canvas of an epic to discover.

Curiosities

  • Realistic Physics

    The game uses an advanced physics engine that affects elements such as weather, temperature and gravity. For example, hot air currents can lift Link with a parasail, and metal weapons will attract lightning during storms.

  • References to Other Zelda

    The game is full of references to other titles in the "Zelda" series. From character names to classic items, fans can enjoy discovering nods to the franchise's rich history.

  • Real Animal Sounds

    The animal sounds in the game were recorded in real life for authenticity. For example, bird sounds were captured in natural locations to realistically reflect the game's environment.
